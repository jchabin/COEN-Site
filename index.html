To me, being an engineer is about problem-solving skills and how they relate to math and science. A large aspect of this is planning and contruction of solutions, whether these solutions are physical, virtual, or just conceptual. The responsibility that comes with the title of Engineer is a guarantee that my work is complete to the extent of my abilities, and in cases where my projects would be relied on for serious applications, it means that my work will do everything it should.

I've had an interest in the problem-solving aspects of engineering for a long time, which is most of the reason I'm majoring in an engineering field.

Meta-Reflection 1

I think the most important concept I took from this class was the Energy Diagram. I haven't actually broken that down into a written list before, and it was interesting to see it all of the information at once. At the time I wrote it, I wasn't able to gain much from the diagram I created, but in retrospect, it showed where I was making things worse for myself, and I've been able to improve things slightly in those areas (just by reoganizing my schedule). These concepts also have applications to the rest of engineering; in a more general interpretation, being able to detail what is and isn't working is essential to the engineering process, and anything where trial-and-error is a step. I think a takeaway from the energy diagram it showed me to stop doing trying things that don't work. Sometimes I have to change something for things to get better.


Meta-Reflection 2

I think the prototyping was helpful in order to make goals more digestable and approachable, and to let me consider completely new ideas that aren't limited by whether I would actually do it. Often I would refuse to really think through a plan if I knew I wasn't going to do it, but by considering it anyway, I can gain a greater perspective on the issue. Sometimes the end result of a plan I didn't consider is still signficiant, even if the steps before aren't. The strategy is also helpful for ideas that I am seriously considering; by breaking down into a detailed, step-by-step plan, it makes it much easier to begin moving toward that goal. I use this specifically for homework a lot, by breaking it into small steps, I feel more able to do the first part, instead of attempting so much at once.
